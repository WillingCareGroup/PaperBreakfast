# Researcher Interest Profile

## Background and Expertise

The researcher holds a PhD in Development, Stem Cell and Regenerative
Medicine and has spent the majority of their doctoral training working
on hematopoietic progenitor biology and cell therapy engineering. The
researcher's primary experimental system has been the Granulocyte-
Macrophage Progenitor (GMP): the researcher led a multi-year program
identifying chemical means of ex vivo GMP expansion, engineered
CAR-GMP and CAR-macrophage platforms for tumor immunotherapy, and
directed IND-enabling allogeneic safety and efficacy studies in
xenograft models.

The researcher has deep, first-hand expertise in: hematopoietic
hierarchy and progenitor biology, CAR architecture design and
optimization, primary cell genetic engineering (lentiviral, retroviral,
CRISPR, transposon, mRNA), in vivo pharmacology in NSG and NBSGW
xenograft models, high-dimensional flow cytometry (20+ color,
spectral), and computational multi-omics (scRNA-seq, ATAC-seq, bulk
RNA-seq, phosphoproteomics). The researcher is also a co-inventor on
two licensed patents that have spawned funded startups.

Assume graduate-level or deeper literacy in all areas above.


## Scientific Interests

### Hematopoietic Stem and Progenitor Cell Expansion

The researcher is broadly interested in how stem and progenitor cells
can be expanded ex vivo while retaining potency. This includes any
biological system capable of giving rise to mature blood or immune
cells — HSCs, HSPCs, GMPs, and any progenitor population including
iPSC-derived equivalents or hypothetical reprogrammed sources. The
researcher cares about the underlying mechanisms (self-renewal
circuitry, niche signals, metabolic state, epigenetic identity) as
much as the practical outcomes. Papers that reveal new biology about
what keeps a stem cell a stem cell, or what drives commitment, are as
interesting as papers that demonstrate expansion.

### Allogeneic Cell Therapy and Immune Rejection

The researcher is interested in the biology of how the immune system
recognizes and rejects foreign cells, and how that rejection can be
circumvented to enable off-the-shelf cellular products. This spans
molecular mechanisms of immune recognition, engineering strategies for
immune evasion, and the clinical and manufacturing challenges of
allogeneic platforms. iPSC-derived immune and hematopoietic products
are of particular interest here.

### Dendritic Cell Biology and Immunotherapy

The researcher has active research interest in dendritic cells, their
subset diversity, their role in orchestrating adaptive immunity, and
their potential as therapeutic vehicles. This includes DC differentiation
from progenitors, DC engineering for enhanced immunostimulatory function,
DC-based vaccines, and DC biology in the tumor microenvironment.

### AI-Assisted Protein and Molecule Design

The researcher follows the rapidly evolving field of computational
protein design closely. This includes generative models for de novo
protein and small molecule design, ML-guided directed evolution,
structure prediction advances, and AI-designed biologics with
therapeutic relevance. The researcher is interested both in
methodological advances and in demonstrated applications.

### AI-Assisted Laboratory Automation

The researcher is interested in the convergence of AI and robotic
laboratory systems: self-driving labs, closed-loop experimental
optimization, active learning applied to biological workflows, and
any platform that meaningfully accelerates or transforms how
biological research is conducted.

### Clinical Cell and Gene Therapy

The researcher wants to stay current with the clinical translation
frontier: first-in-human results, pivotal trial readouts, regulatory
milestones, and any therapeutic development that represents a
meaningful step forward in cell or gene therapy. This includes but
is not limited to CAR-T and CAR-NK advances, gene therapy for
monogenic blood disorders, and HSCT innovations.
